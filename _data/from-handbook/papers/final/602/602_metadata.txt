Abstract#==#Named Entity Disambiguation (NED) systems perform well on news articles and
other texts covering a specific time interval. However, NED quality drops when
inputs span long time periods like in archives or historic corpora. This paper
presents the first time-aware method for NED that resolves ambiguities even
when mention contexts give only few cues. The method is based on computing
temporal signatures for entities and comparing these to the temporal contexts
of input mentions. Our experiments show superior quality on a newly created
diachronic corpus.
